     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Scottish Government
      </b>
      (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic_language" title="Scottish Gaelic language">
       Scottish Gaelic
      </a>
      :
      <span lang="gd" xml:lang="gd">
       <i>
        Riaghaltas na h-Alba
       </i>
      </span>
      ;
      <a href="/wiki/Scots_language" title="Scots language">
       Scots
      </a>
      :
      <span lang="sco" xml:lang="sco">
       <i>
        Scots Govrenment
       </i>
      </span>
      ) is the
      <a href="/wiki/Executive_(government)" title="Executive (government)">
       executive
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/Devolution_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Devolution in the United Kingdom">
       devolved
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament" title="Scottish Parliament">
       Scottish Parliament
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dwe3_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-dwe3-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The government was established in 1999 as the
      <b>
       Scottish Executive
      </b>
      under section 44(1) of the
      <a href="/wiki/Scotland_Act_1998" title="Scotland Act 1998">
       Scotland Act 1998
      </a>
      , which created a devolved administration for
      <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">
       Scotland
      </a>
      in line with the result of the
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_devolution_referendum,_1997" title="Scottish devolution referendum, 1997">
       1997 referendum on Scottish devolution
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-part2_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-part2-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The government consists of cabinet secretaries, who attend cabinet meetings, and ministers, who do not. It is led by the
      <a href="/wiki/First_Minister_of_Scotland" title="First Minister of Scotland">
       first minister
      </a>
      , who selects the cabinet secretaries and ministers with approval of parliament.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-First_Minister_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-First_Minister-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cabinet_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-cabinet-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Responsibilities">
       Responsibilities
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Responsibilities">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Scottish Government is responsible for devolved matters, and those not explicitly reserved to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Parliament" title="British Parliament">
       British Parliament
      </a>
      in Westminster, by Schedule 5 to the
      <a href="/wiki/Scotland_Act_1998" title="Scotland Act 1998">
       Scotland Act 1998
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Devolved matters that were decided on by the Scotland Act 1998 included
      <a href="/wiki/NHS_Scotland" title="NHS Scotland">
       healthcare provision
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Scotland" title="Education in Scotland">
       education
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Scots_law" title="Scots law">
       justice
      </a>
      , policing, rural affairs, economic development and
      <a href="/wiki/Transport_in_Scotland" title="Transport in Scotland">
       transport
      </a>
      . The Scottish Government also has administrative responsibility for some matters where it does not have legislative power. An example is Sections 36 &amp; 37 of the Electricity Act 1989 which allow the Scottish Government to authorise power transmission lines and grant power generation consents.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the aftermath of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_Independence_Referendum,_2014" title="Scottish Independence Referendum, 2014">
       Scottish independence Referendum
      </a>
      in 2014, the Smith Commission was established to decide upon what matters should further be devolved given the increased hunger of the Scottish people for home rule. Some matters that were decided upon for devolution were some elements of Social Security, policing of transport, the Crown Estate in Scotland, road signage and speed limits and further elements of taxation.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Scottish Government's budget is decided upon by the block grant that is formulated using the Barnett Formula with the ability to also increase or decrease income tax rates. In the financial year of 2016-17, the government's annual budget was
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pound_Sterling" title="Pound Sterling">
       £
      </a>
      37.2 billion.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-about-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The government is led by the
      <a href="/wiki/First_Minister_of_Scotland" title="First Minister of Scotland">
       First Minister
      </a>
      . The
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament" title="Scottish Parliament">
       Scottish Parliament
      </a>
      nominates one of its members to be appointed as first minister by
      <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Monarchy of the United Kingdom">
       the monarch
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fasjj32_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Fasjj32-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      He or she is assisted by various cabinet secretaries with individual
      <a href="/wiki/Ministry_(government_department)" title="Ministry (government department)">
       portfolios
      </a>
      , who are appointed by him/her with the approval of parliament. Ministers are similarly appointed to assist cabinet secretaries in their work. The Scottish law officers, the
      <a href="/wiki/Lord_Advocate" title="Lord Advocate">
       Lord Advocate
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Solicitor_General_for_Scotland" title="Solicitor General for Scotland">
       Solicitor General
      </a>
      , can be appointed from outside the parliament's membership, but are subject to its approval. They are appointed by the monarch on the recommendation of the first minister.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fasjj32_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Fasjj32-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The first minister, the cabinet secretaries and the Scottish law officers are the members of the Scottish Government. They are collectively known as the "Scottish Ministers".
     </p>
     <p>
      The members of the government have substantial influence over
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Acts_of_the_Scottish_Parliament_from_1999" title="List of Acts of the Scottish Parliament from 1999">
       legislation
      </a>
      in Scotland, putting forward the majority of bills that are successful in becoming Acts of the Scottish Parliament.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-works_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-works-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cabinet_secretaries_and_ministers">
       Cabinet secretaries and ministers
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cabinet secretaries and ministers">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      This article is about the current Scottish Government. For previous ones, see
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Scottish_Governments" title="List of Scottish Governments">
       List of Scottish Governments
      </a>
      .
     </div>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      "Scottish ministers" redirects here. For the pre-1999 use of the term, see
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Office" title="Scottish Office">
       Scottish Office
      </a>
      .
     </div>
     <p>
      The structure of the ministerial team used by the
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_National_Party" title="Scottish National Party">
       Scottish National Party
      </a>
      (SNP) after its
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament_general_election_2007" title="Scottish Parliament general election 2007">
       election victory in May 2007
      </a>
      differs from those used by previous governments. The title
      <i>
       cabinet secretary

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cabinet">
       Cabinet
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cabinet">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Scottish_Cabinet,_May_2011.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="366" data-file-width="550" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Scottish_Cabinet%2C_May_2011.jpg/300px-Scottish_Cabinet%2C_May_2011.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Scottish_Cabinet%2C_May_2011.jpg/450px-Scottish_Cabinet%2C_May_2011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/85/Scottish_Cabinet%2C_May_2011.jpg 2x" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Scottish_Cabinet,_May_2011.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The previous Scottish Cabinet (under Alex Salmond).
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Scottish Cabinet
      </b>
      is the group of ministers who are collectively responsible for all Scottish Government policy. While parliament is in session, the cabinet meets weekly.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Normally meetings are held on Tuesday afternoons in
      <a href="/wiki/Bute_House" title="Bute House">
       Bute House
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Official_residence" title="Official residence">
       official residence
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/First_Minister_of_Scotland" title="First Minister of Scotland">
       first minister
      </a>
      . The cabinet consists of the cabinet secretaries, excluding the Scottish Law Officers (the
      <a href="/wiki/Lord_Advocate" title="Lord Advocate">
       Lord Advocate
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Solicitor_General_for_Scotland" title="Solicitor General for Scotland">
       Solicitor General
      </a>
      ). The Lord Advocate attends meetings of the cabinet only when requested by the first minister, and he is not formally a member.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-excluded_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-excluded-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The cabinet is supported by the Cabinet Secretariat, which is based at
      <a href="/wiki/St_Andrew%27s_House" title="St Andrew's House">
       St Andrew's House
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cabinet_sub-committees">
       Cabinet sub-committees
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cabinet sub-committees">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      There are currently two sub-committees of Cabinet:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-subcommittees_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-subcommittees-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Cabinet Sub-Committee on
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Acts_of_the_Scottish_Parliament_from_1999" title="List of Acts of the Scottish Parliament from 1999">
        Legislation
       </a>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Membership: the Deputy First Minister and Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing, the Minister for Parliamentary Business, and the Lord Advocate.
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government_Resilience_Room" title="Scottish Government Resilience Room">
        Scottish Government Resilience Room
       </a>
       (SGoRR) Cabinet Sub-Committee
       <ul>
        <li>
         Membership: Cabinet Secretary for Justice (Chair), the Cabinet Secretary for Finance and Sustainable Growth, the Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing,the Cabinet Secretary for Rural Affairs and the Environment and the Lord Advocate.
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      For several years prior to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glasgow_2014_Commonwealth_Games" title="Glasgow 2014 Commonwealth Games">
       Glasgow 2014 Commonwealth Games
      </a>
      there had been a third sub-committee of Cabinet:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Glasgow 2014 Legacy Plan Delivery Group
       <ul>
        <li>
         Membership: Deputy First Minister and Cabinet Secretary for Health and Wellbeing (Chair), Minister for Community Safety, Minister for Culture, External Affairs and the Constitution, Minister for Enterprise, Energy and Tourism, Minister for Environment, Minister for Housing and Communities, Minister for Public Health and Sport, Minister for Schools and Skills, and the Minister for Transport, Infrastructure and Climate Change.
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_service">
       Civil service
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Civil service">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Scottish Government also includes a civil service that supports the Scottish ministers. According to 2012 reports, there are 16,000 civil servants working in core Scottish Government directorates and agencies.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-service_15-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-service-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The civil service is a matter
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reserved_matter" title="Reserved matter">
       reserved
      </a>
      to the
      <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">
       British parliament
      </a>
      at Westminster (rather than
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Devolved" title="Devolved">
       devolved
      </a>
      to
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament" title="Scottish Parliament">
       Holyrood
      </a>
      ): Scottish Government civil servants work within the rules and customs of
      <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Civil_Service" title="Her Majesty's Civil Service">
       Her Majesty's Civil Service
      </a>
      , but serve the devolved administration rather than
      <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Government of the United Kingdom">
       British government
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-faq_16-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-faq-16">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Permanent_secretary">
       Permanent secretary
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Permanent secretary">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Office_of_the_Permanent_Secretary_(Scotland)" title="Office of the Permanent Secretary (Scotland)">
       Office of the Permanent Secretary (Scotland)
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Permanent_secretary" title="Permanent secretary">
       permanent secretary
      </a>
      is the most senior Scottish civil servant, leads the strategic board, and supports the
      <a href="/wiki/First_Minister_of_Scotland" title="First Minister of Scotland">
       first minister
      </a>
      and cabinet. The current permanent secretary is
      <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Evans" title="Leslie Evans">
       Leslie Evans
      </a>
      , who assumed the post in July 2015.
     </p>
     <p>
      The permanent secretary is a member of the
      <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Civil_Service" title="Her Majesty's Civil Service">
       Her Majesty's Civil Service
      </a>
      , and therefore takes part in the permanent secretaries management group of the Civil Service
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-permanent_17-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-permanent-17">
        [17]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and is answerable to the most senior civil servant in Britain, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cabinet_secretary" title="Cabinet secretary">
       cabinet secretary
      </a>
      , for his or her professional conduct. He or she remains, however, at the direction of the Scottish ministers.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Directorates">
       Directorates
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Directorates">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Directorates_of_the_Scottish_Government" title="Directorates of the Scottish Government">
       Directorates of the Scottish Government
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      "Directorates" are the
      <a href="/wiki/Ministry_(government_department)" title="Ministry (government department)">
       ministries
      </a>
      of the Scottish Government. They serve to execute government policy. Unlike in the
      <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Government of the United Kingdom">
       British government
      </a>
      , cabinet secretaries do not lead the directorates, and have no direct role in their operation. Instead, the directorates are grouped together into six "Directorates General", each run by a senior civil servant who is titled a "Director-General". As of May 2016, there are six Directorates General:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Learning_and_Justice_Directorates" title="Learning and Justice Directorates">
        Learning and Justice Directorates
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Health_and_Social_Care_Directorates" title="Health and Social Care Directorates">
        Health and Social Care Directorates
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Economy_Directorates" title="Economy Directorates">
        Economy Directorates
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Finance_Directorates" title="Finance Directorates">
        Finance Directorates
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governance_and_Communities_Directorates" title="Governance and Communities Directorates">
        Communities Directorates
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strategy_and_Operations_Directorates" title="Strategy and Operations Directorates">
        Strategy and Operations Directorates
       </a>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Supporting these directorates are a variety of other corporate service teams and professional groups.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-directorates_18-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-directorates-18">
        [18]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Crown_Office_and_Procurator_Fiscal_Service" title="Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service">
       Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service
      </a>
      serves as an independent
      <a href="/wiki/Prosecutor" title="Prosecutor">
       prosecution service
      </a>
      in Scotland, and is a ministerial department of the Scottish Government. It is headed by the
      <a href="/wiki/Lord_Advocate" title="Lord Advocate">
       Lord Advocate
      </a>
      , who is responsible for prosecution, along with the
      <a href="/wiki/Procurator_fiscal" title="Procurator fiscal">
       procurators fiscal
      </a>
      , under
      <a href="/wiki/Scots_law" title="Scots law">
       Scots law
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Strategic_board">
       Strategic board
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Strategic board">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strategic_board_of_the_Scottish_Government" title="Strategic board of the Scottish Government">
       strategic board
      </a>
      is composed of the permanent secretary, the six directors-general, two chief advisers (scientific and economic) and four non-executive directors. The board is responsible for providing support to the government through the permanent secretary, and is the executive of the Scottish civil service.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SB_19-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-SB-19">
        [19]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Executive_agencies">
       Executive agencies
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Executive agencies">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Executive_agencies_of_the_Scottish_Government" title="Executive agencies of the Scottish Government">
       Executive agencies of the Scottish Government
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      To deliver its work, there are 8
      <a href="/wiki/Executive_agencies_of_the_Scottish_Government" title="Executive agencies of the Scottish Government">
       executive agencies
      </a>
      established by ministers as part of government departments, or as departments in their own right, to carry out a discrete area of work. These include, for example, the
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Prison_Service" title="Scottish Prison Service">
       Scottish Prison Service
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Transport_Scotland" title="Transport Scotland">
       Transport Scotland
      </a>
      . Executive agencies are staffed by civil servants.
     </p>
     <p>
      There are two non-ministerial departments that form part of the Scottish administration, and therefore the devolved administration, but answer directly to the Scottish Parliament rather than to ministers: these are the
      <a href="/wiki/General_Register_Office_for_Scotland" title="General Register Office for Scotland">
       General Register Office for Scotland
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Office_of_the_Scottish_Charity_Regulator" title="Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator">
       Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Public_bodies">
       Public bodies
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Public bodies">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_public_bodies" title="Scottish public bodies">
       Scottish public bodies
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The Scottish Government is also responsible for a large number of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_public_bodies" title="Scottish public bodies">
       non-departmental public bodies
      </a>
      . These include executive NDPBs (e.g.
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Enterprise" title="Scottish Enterprise">
       Scottish Enterprise
      </a>
      ); advisory NDPBs (e.g. the
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Law_Commission" title="Scottish Law Commission">
       Scottish Law Commission
      </a>
      );
      <a href="/wiki/Tribunal" title="Tribunal">
       tribunals
      </a>
      (e.g. the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Children%27s_Panel" title="Children's Panel">
       Children's Panel
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Additional_Support_Needs_Tribunals_for_Scotland" title="Additional Support Needs Tribunals for Scotland">
       Additional Support Needs Tribunals for Scotland
      </a>
      ); and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nationalised_industries" title="Nationalised industries">
       nationalised industries
      </a>
      (e.g.
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Water" title="Scottish Water">
       Scottish Water
      </a>
      ). These are staffed by public servants, rather than civil servants.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Scottish Government is also responsible for some other public bodies that are not classed as non-departmental public bodies, such as
      <a href="/wiki/NHS_Scotland" title="NHS Scotland">
       NHS Boards
      </a>
      , Visiting Committees for Scottish Penal Establishments or HM Chief Inspector of Constabulary for Scotland.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Offices">
       Offices
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Offices">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The main building of the Scottish Government is
      <a href="/wiki/St_Andrew%27s_House" title="St Andrew's House">
       St Andrew's House
      </a>
      , which is located on
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calton_Hill,_Edinburgh" title="Calton Hill, Edinburgh">
       Calton Hill
      </a>
      in Edinburgh. Some other government departments are based at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victoria_Quay,_Scotland" title="Victoria Quay, Scotland">
       Victoria Quay
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Leith" title="Leith">
       Leith
      </a>
      , Saughton House on Broomhouse Drive, and Atlantic Quay on Broomielaw, Glasgow. The Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service has its head offices, and the Lord Advocate's Chambers, at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chambers_Street_(Edinburgh)" title="Chambers Street (Edinburgh)">
       Chambers Street
      </a>
      in central Edinburgh.
     </p>
     <p>
      There are numerous other Edinburgh properties occupied by the Scottish Government. The Security Branch is based in the old Governor's House on the site of the former Calton Gaol, next door to St Andrew's House on Regent Road. The Government Car Service for Scotland also has its Edinburgh offices on Bonnington Road, in Leith. Other offices are scattered around central Edinburgh, including
      <a href="/wiki/Bute_House" title="Bute House">
       Bute House
      </a>
      on Charlotte Square, the official residence of the first minister.
     </p>
     <p>
      New St Andrew's House, above and behind Edinburgh's St James' Centre, was once a large
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Office" title="Scottish Office">
       Scottish Office
      </a>
      building, which was occupied from 1973 until 1997, when the last remaining staff moved to Victoria Quay.
     </p>
     <p>
      The first minister has use of the
      <a href="/wiki/Scotland_Office" title="Scotland Office">
       Scotland Office
      </a>
      building,
      <a href="/wiki/Dover_House" title="Dover House">
       Dover House
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Whitehall" title="Whitehall">
       Whitehall
      </a>
      when necessary.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dover_20-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-dover-20">
        [20]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Scottish Government has a
      <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">
       European Union
      </a>
      representative office, located at Rond-Point Robert Schuman in
      <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">
       Brussels
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">
       Belgium
      </a>
      , which forms a part of the United Kingdom Permanent Representation to the European Union.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eu_21-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-eu-21">
        [21]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Scottish Government also maintains an office within the British Embassy in Washington, D.C., and has accredited representatives within the British Embassy in Beijing.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Change_of_name">
       Change of name
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Government&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Change of name">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Scottish_Executive_logo_(bilingual).png">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="83" data-file-width="375" height="66" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/73/Scottish_Executive_logo_%28bilingual%29.png/300px-Scottish_Executive_logo_%28bilingual%29.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/7/73/Scottish_Executive_logo_%28bilingual%29.png 1.5x" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Scottish_Executive_logo_(bilingual).png" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The Scottish Executive's original logo, shown with English and
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic_language" title="Scottish Gaelic language">
         Scottish Gaelic
        </a>
        caption. The logo was replaced in September 2007, with the name changed to "Scottish Government", and the
        <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Scotland" title="Flag of Scotland">
         Flag of Scotland
        </a>
        used instead of the Royal Arms. The original logo is still used by the
        <a href="/wiki/Scotland_Office" title="Scotland Office">
         Scotland Office
        </a>
        .
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The original Scotland Act 1998 gave the name "Scottish Executive" as the legal term for the devolved government. In January 2001, the then First Minister
      <a href="/wiki/Henry_McLeish" title="Henry McLeish">
       Henry McLeish
      </a>
      suggested changing the official name from "Scottish Executive" to "Scottish Government". The reaction from the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_government" title="British government">
       British government
      </a>
      and from some
      <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">
       Labour Party
      </a>
      members and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_Labour" title="Scottish Labour">
       Scottish Labour
      </a>
      MPs was allegedly hostile.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britten_22-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-britten-22">
        [22]
       </a>
      </sup>
      This reaction was in contrast to a 2001 public survey by then-Labour chief whip
      <a href="/wiki/Tom_McCabe" title="Tom McCabe">
       Tom McCabe
      </a>
      , which showed that only 29% of the Scottish public wanted the title Scottish Executive to remain.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcrename_23-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-bbcrename-23">
        [23]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Scottish politicians, including the Labour first minister, had often referred to the executive as the "government" and this trend increased following the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament_general_election_2007" title="Scottish Parliament general election 2007">
       2007 election
      </a>
      , when the SNP took office and Labour were in opposition for the first time. On 2 September 2007, the SNP minority government announced that the Scottish Executive was to be re-branded as the "Scottish Government".
     </p>
     <p>
      The renaming was decided unilaterally by the minority government; as a consequence, the SNP was criticised by the three
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unionism_(Scotland)" title="Unionism (Scotland)">
       Unionist
      </a>
      opposition parties for acting without allowing for parliamentary scrutiny, debate or approval of their plan. However, the term "Scottish Government" has since then become common currency among all of the political parties in Scotland and the rest of the UK.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        [24]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The official Gaelic title,
      <i>
       Riaghaltas na h-Alba
      </i>
      , has always meant "Government of Scotland".
     </p>
     <p>
      "Scottish Executive" remained the legal name under section 44(1) of the
      <a href="/wiki/Scotland_Act_1998" title="Scotland Act 1998">
       Scotland Act 1998
      </a>
      until 2 July 2012. Neither the Scottish Executive nor the Scottish Parliament were able to change the legal name, as this required the British parliament to amend the Scotland Act. Section 12(1) of the
      <a href="/wiki/Scotland_Act_2012" title="Scotland Act 2012">
       Scotland Act 2012
      </a>
      , which came into effect on 3 July 2012, formally changed the name of the Scottish Executive to the "Scottish Government".
     </p>
     <p>
      At the same time that the Scottish Government began to use its new name, a new emblem was adopted. The earlier version featured the old name and a version of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Coat_of_Arms_of_the_United_Kingdom#Scotland" title="Royal Coat of Arms of the United Kingdom">
       Royal Arms for Scotland
      </a>
      , but without the
      <a href="/wiki/Motto" title="Motto">
       motto
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Helmet" title="Helmet">
       helm
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Mantling" title="Mantling">
       mantling
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Crest_(heraldry)" title="Crest (heraldry)">
       crest
      </a>
      , the war-cry above the crest, or the flags of
      <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Scotland" title="Flag of Scotland">
       Scotland
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_England" title="Flag of England">
       England
      </a>
      carried by the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supporters" title="Supporters">
       supporters
      </a>
      . In the rendering used, both supporters appeared to be crowned with the
      <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Scotland" title="Crown of Scotland">
       Crown of Scotland
      </a>
      , whereas in the Royal Arms, the Scottish
      <a href="/wiki/Unicorn" title="Unicorn">
       unicorn
      </a>
      is usually shown crowned with the Scottish Crown, and the English lion with
      <a href="/wiki/St_Edward%27s_Crown" title="St Edward's Crown">
       St Edward's Crown
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In the September 2007 rebranding, this depiction of the
      <a href="/wiki/Royal_coat_of_arms_of_Scotland" title="Royal coat of arms of Scotland">
       Royal Arms
      </a>
      was replaced by one of the
      <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Scotland" title="Flag of Scotland">
       Flag of Scotland
      </a>
      . However, the Royal Arms are still used by the Government for some official documents, such as directions issued in exercise of powers provided by legislation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aib_25-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-aib-25">
        [25]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2016, a refreshed version of the Scottish Government logo was launched and used on all government websites and letters of correspondence.
     </p>

     <p>

      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Labour_Party" title="Scottish Labour Party">

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Liberal_Democrats" title="Scottish Liberal Democrats">

      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_National_Party" title="Scottish National Party">
       Scottish National Party
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Sturgeon" title="Nicola Sturgeon">

      </a>
