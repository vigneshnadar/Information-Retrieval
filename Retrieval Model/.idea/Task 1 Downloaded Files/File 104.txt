     <p>
      <b>
       Strangford Lough
      </b>
      (from
      <a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">
       Old Norse
      </a>
      <i>
       Strangr Fjörðr
      </i>
      , meaning "strong sea-inlet"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PlaceNames_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-PlaceNames-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      ) is a large sea
      <a href="/wiki/Loch" title="Loch">
       loch
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Inlet" title="Inlet">
       inlet
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/County_Down" title="County Down">
       County Down
      </a>
      , in the east of
      <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">
       Northern Ireland
      </a>
      . It is the largest inlet in the
      <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">
       British Isles
      </a>
      , covering 150 km
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      . The loch is almost totally enclosed by the
      <a href="/wiki/Ards_Peninsula" title="Ards Peninsula">
       Ards Peninsula
      </a>
      and is linked to the
      <a href="/wiki/Irish_Sea" title="Irish Sea">
       Irish Sea
      </a>
      by a long narrow channel or strait. The main body of the loch has at least seventy islands along with many islets (pladdies), bays, coves, headlands and mudflats. Strangford Lough was designated as Northern Ireland's first
      <a href="/wiki/Marine_Conservation_Zone" title="Marine Conservation Zone">
       Marine Conservation Zone
      </a>
      (MCZ) under the introduction of the Marine Act (Northern Ireland) 2013. It has also been designated a
      <a href="/wiki/Special_Area_of_Conservation" title="Special Area of Conservation">
       Special Area of Conservation
      </a>
      under the EU
      <a href="/wiki/Habitats_Directive" title="Habitats Directive">
       Habitats Directive
      </a>
      , and its abundant wildlife is recognised internationally for its importance.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the medieval and early modern period Strangford Lough was known in Irish as
      <b>
       Loch Cuan
      </b>
      , meaning "sea-inlet of bays/havens".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PlaceNames_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-PlaceNames-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Strangford Lough is a popular tourist destination noted for its fishing and scenery. Towns and villages around the loch include
      <a href="/wiki/Killyleagh" title="Killyleagh">
       Killyleagh
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Comber" title="Comber">
       Comber
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Newtownards" title="Newtownards">
       Newtownards
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Portaferry" title="Portaferry">
       Portaferry
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Strangford" title="Strangford">
       Strangford
      </a>
      . The latter two are connected by a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RORO" title="RORO">
       car ferry
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Name">
       Name
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangford_Lough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The name
      <i>
       Strangford
      </i>
      comes from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Norse_language" title="Old Norse language">
       Old Norse
      </a>
      <i>
       Strangr-fjörðr
      </i>
      , meaning "strong sea-inlet".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pni_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pni-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Vikings" title="Vikings">
       Vikings
      </a>
      were active in the area during the Middle Ages. Originally, this name referred only to the narrow channel linking the loch to the sea (between the villages of
      <a href="/wiki/Strangford" title="Strangford">
       Strangford
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Portaferry" title="Portaferry">
       Portaferry
      </a>
      ).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pni_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-pni-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Up until about the 18th century, the main body of the loch was better known by the (older)
      <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">
       Irish
      </a>
      name
      <i>
       Loch Cuan
      </i>
      , meaning "loch of the bays/havens".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pni_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-pni-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      This name was anglicized as
      <i>
       Lough Coan
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Lough Cone
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Lough Coyn
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Lough Coin
      </i>
      , or similar.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pni_2-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-pni-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The narrow channel may have been known in Latin as the
      <i>
       fretum Brene
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In
      <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Scots_dialects" title="Ulster Scots dialects">
       Ulster-Scots
      </a>
      the loch's name is spelt
      <i>
       Strangfurd
      </i>
      or
      <i>
       Strangﬁrt Loch
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">
       Flora and fauna
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangford_Lough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Flora">
       Flora
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangford_Lough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flora">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Common cord-grass (
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Spartina_anglica" title="Spartina anglica">
        Spartina anglica
       </a>
      </i>
      ) C.E. Hubbard was introduced in the mid-1940s is now abundant.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Maerl" title="Maerl">
       Maerl
      </a>
      is a calcareous deposit, in the main, of two species, of
      <a href="/wiki/Calcareous" title="Calcareous">
       calcareous
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Algae" title="Algae">
       algae
      </a>
      <i>
       Phymatolithon calcareum
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Lithothamnion glaciale
      </i>
      which form free-living beds of unattached, branched
      <a href="/wiki/Coralline_algae" title="Coralline algae">
       corallines
      </a>
      , living or dead, in Strangford Lough.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The rocky and boulder shores toward the south of the lough are dominated by the seaweed knotted wrack
      <i>
       Ascophyllum nodosum
      </i>
      . The usual zonation of weeds on these shore is, at the top channel wrack (
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pelvetia" title="Pelvetia">
        Pelvetia
       </a>
       canaliculata
      </i>
      (L.) Dcne. et Rhur.), followed by spiral wrack (
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Fucus_spiralis" title="Fucus spiralis">
        Fucus spiralis
       </a>
      </i>
      L.), then knotted wrack (
      <i>
       Ascophyllum nodosum
      </i>
      (L.) Le Jol) with some admixture of bladder wrack (
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Fucus_vesiculosus" title="Fucus vesiculosus">
        Fucus vesiculosus
       </a>
      </i>
      ) L. and then serrated wrack (
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Fucus_serratus" title="Fucus serratus">
        Fucus serratus
       </a>
      </i>
      L.) before coming to the low water kelps.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      A brown
      <a href="/wiki/Seaweed" title="Seaweed">
       seaweed
      </a>
      named
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Sargassum" title="Sargassum">
        Sargassum
       </a>
       muticum
      </i>
      , originally from the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific" title="Pacific">
       Pacific
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
       Japan
      </a>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Fauna">
       Fauna
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangford_Lough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Fauna">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Strangford Lough and Islands is an
      <a href="/wiki/Important_Bird_Area" title="Important Bird Area">
       Important Bird Area
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Strangford Lough is an important winter
      <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">
       migration
      </a>
      destination for many wading and sea
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birds" title="Birds">
       birds
      </a>
      .
      <a href="/wiki/Animal" title="Animal">
       Animals
      </a>
      commonly found in the lough include
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Common_seal" title="Common seal">
       common seals
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Basking_shark" title="Basking shark">
       basking sharks
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brent_goose" title="Brent goose">
       brent geese
      </a>
      . Three quarters of the world population of pale bellied brent geese spend winter in the lough area.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The invasive carpet sea squirt,
      <i>
       Didemnum vexillum
      </i>
      , was found in the Lough in 2012.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Tidal_electricity">
       Tidal electricity
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangford_Lough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tidal electricity">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SeaGen_installed.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="300" data-file-width="358" height="184" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/SeaGen_installed.jpg/220px-SeaGen_installed.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/SeaGen_installed.jpg/330px-SeaGen_installed.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/77/SeaGen_installed.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SeaGen_installed.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a href="/wiki/SeaGen" title="SeaGen">
         SeaGen
        </a>
        .
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      In 2007 Strangford Lough became home to the world's first commercial
      <a href="/wiki/Marine_science" title="Marine science">
       tidal stream
      </a>
      power station,
      <a href="/wiki/SeaGen" title="SeaGen">
       SeaGen
      </a>
      . The 1.2
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megawatt" title="Megawatt">
       megawatt
      </a>
      underwater tidal electricity generator, part of Northern Ireland's
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Environment_and_Renewable_Energy_Fund&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Environment and Renewable Energy Fund (page does not exist)">
       Environment and Renewable Energy Fund
      </a>
      scheme, takes advantage of the fast tidal flow in the lough which can be up to 4
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M/s" title="M/s">
       m/s
      </a>
      . Although the generator is powerful enough to power up to a thousand homes, the
      <a href="/wiki/Turbine" title="Turbine">
       turbine
      </a>
      has a minimal
      <a href="/wiki/Environmental_degradation" title="Environmental degradation">
       environmental impact
      </a>
      , as it is almost entirely submerged, and the rotors turn slowly enough that they pose no danger to
      <a href="/wiki/Wildlife" title="Wildlife">
       wildlife
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        [17]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Since June 2008 a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tidal_energy" title="Tidal energy">
       tidal energy
      </a>
      device called
      <a href="/wiki/Evopod" title="Evopod">
       Evopod
      </a>
      has been tested in Strangford Lough near the Portaferry Ferry landing.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        [18]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The device is a 1/10 scale prototype and is being monitored by
      <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_University_Belfast" title="Queen's University Belfast">
       Queen's University Belfast
      </a>
      . The device is a semi submerged floating tidal turbine and is moored to the seabed via a buoy mounted swivel so that it always maintains optimum heading into the direction of the tidal flow. The scale device is not grid connected and dissipates the small amount of power it generates as heat into the sea. Malik Priestley (executive creator of SeaGen) said "tidal power will provide sustainable amounts of green, renewable energy and pose no threats to surrounding wild life".
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Activity_on_Strangford_Lough">
       Activity on Strangford Lough
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Strangford_(16),_August_2009.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2848" data-file-width="4272" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Strangford_%2816%29%2C_August_2009.JPG/220px-Strangford_%2816%29%2C_August_2009.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Strangford_%2816%29%2C_August_2009.JPG/330px-Strangford_%2816%29%2C_August_2009.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Strangford_%2816%29%2C_August_2009.JPG/440px-Strangford_%2816%29%2C_August_2009.JPG 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Strangford_(16),_August_2009.JPG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Strangford Lough Canoe Trail, August 2009
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Strangford Lough is a place of natural beauty that the locals put to great use. From using it as a national highway (Portaferry-Strangford ferry) to using it for activities of leisure - sailing, kayaking, bird watching, diving and much more. There are a number of companies based in the local area encouraging visitors to explore the area including: Exploris Aquarium - where you can learn more about the lough and its wildlife; Clearsky Adventure Centre - kayaking, canoeing etc. and DV diving. There are also canoe trails in place and a 'Strangford Lough Activity Map' has been launched as a part of a series of maps produced by the Ordnance Survey of Northern Ireland.
     </p>
     <p>
      In July 2016 the Strangford Lough and Lecale Partnership, Scottish Coastal Rowing Association,
      <a href="/wiki/Newry,_Mourne_and_Down_District_Council" title="Newry, Mourne and Down District Council">
       Newry, Mourne and Down District Council
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Ards_and_North_Down_Borough_Council" title="Ards and North Down Borough Council">
       Ards and North Down Borough Council
      </a>
      , together with local rowing clubs based on Strangford Lough and the
      <a href="/wiki/Ards_Peninsula" title="Ards Peninsula">
       Ards Peninsula
      </a>
      hosted the world community, coastal rowing championships “Skiffie Worlds 2016”. The event was attended by 50 clubs from Scotland, England, Northern Ireland, the Netherlands, The United States, Canada and Tasmania. Racing was held over a 2km course on Strangford Lough at
      <a href="/wiki/Delamont_Country_Park" title="Delamont Country Park">
       Delamont Country Park
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        [19]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <p>
      There has been a ferry service between
      <a href="/wiki/Portaferry" title="Portaferry">
       Portaferry
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Strangford" title="Strangford">

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ferry_20-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ferry-20">
        [20]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The alternative road journey is 47 miles and takes about an hour and a half, while the ferry crosses the 0.6 nautical miles (0.69 mi) in 8 minutes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        [21]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The subsidised public service operates at a loss of more than £1m per year but is viewed as an important transport link to the Ards Peninsula.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        [22]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      MV
      <i>
       Portaferry
      </i>
      (1) (ex-
      <i>
       Cleddau King
      </i>
      ) was bought from a company in Wales in 1975 after completion of the
      <a href="/wiki/Cleddau_Bridge" title="Cleddau Bridge">
       Cleddau Bridge
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        [23]
       </a>
      </sup>
      She was modified by
      <a href="/wiki/Harland_and_Wolff" title="Harland and Wolff">
       Harland and Wolff
      </a>
      of Belfast
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ferry_20-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-ferry-20">
        [20]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and operated until sold in May 2002.
     </p>
     <p>
      MV
      <i>
       Strangford
      </i>
      was built by the Verolme Shipyard in
      <a href="/wiki/Cork_(city)" title="Cork (city)">
       Cork
      </a>
      and launched on 6 September 1969.
     </p>

      <a href="/wiki/MV_Portaferry_II" title="MV Portaferry II">
       MV
       <i>
        Portaferry II
       </i>
      </a>
      , a £2.7 million vessel built by
      <a href="/wiki/Remontowa" title="Remontowa">

      <a href="/wiki/McTay_Marine" title="McTay Marine">

     <h2>

      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tide_mill" title="Tide mill">
       tidal mills
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Kelp" title="Kelp">

      </a>
      walls and harbours and landing places.
