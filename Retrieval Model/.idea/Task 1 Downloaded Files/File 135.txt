     <p>
      The
      <b>
       United Nations Environment Programme
      </b>
      (
      <b>
       UNEP
      </b>
      ) is an agency of United Nations and coordinates its environmental activities, assisting developing countries in implementing
      <a href="/wiki/Environmental_peacebuilding" title="Environmental peacebuilding">
       environmentally sound policies and practices
      </a>
      . It was founded by
      <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Strong" title="Maurice Strong">
       Maurice Strong
      </a>
      , its first director, as a result of the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Conference_on_the_Human_Environment" title="United Nations Conference on the Human Environment">
       United Nations Conference on the Human Environment
      </a>
      (Stockholm Conference) in June 1972 and has its
      <a href="/wiki/Headquarters" title="Headquarters">
       headquarters
      </a>
      in the Gigiri neighborhood of
      <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">
       Nairobi
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">
       Kenya
      </a>
      . UNEP also has six regional offices and various country offices.
     </p>
     <p>
      UNEP has overall responsibility for environmental problems among United Nations agencies but talks on addressing global warming are overseen by the Bonn-based Secretariat of the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Framework_Convention_on_Climate_Change" title="United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change">
       United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Its activities cover a wide range of issues regarding the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth%27s_atmosphere" title="Earth's atmosphere">
       atmosphere
      </a>
      , marine and terrestrial
      <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">
       ecosystems
      </a>
      , environmental governance and green economy. It has played a significant role in developing international environmental conventions, promoting
      <a href="/wiki/Environmental_science" title="Environmental science">
       environmental science
      </a>
      and information and illustrating the way those can be implemented in conjunction with policy, working on the development and implementation of policy with national governments, regional institutions in conjunction with environmental non-governmental organizations (NGOs). UNEP has also been active in funding and implementing environment related development projects.
     </p>
     <p>
      UNEP has aided in the formulation of guidelines and treaties on issues such as the international trade in potentially harmful chemicals, transboundary
      <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">
       air pollution
      </a>
      , and contamination of international waterways.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">
       World Meteorological Organization
      </a>
      and UNEP established the
      <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_Panel_on_Climate_Change" title="Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change">
       Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change
      </a>
      (IPCC) in 1988. UNEP is also one of several Implementing Agencies for the
      <a href="/wiki/Global_Environment_Facility" title="Global Environment Facility">
       Global Environment Facility
      </a>
      (GEF) and the Multilateral Fund for the Implementation of the
      <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Protocol" title="Montreal Protocol">
       Montreal Protocol
      </a>
      , and it is also a member of the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Development_Group" title="United Nations Development Group">
       United Nations Development Group
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/International_Cyanide_Management_Code" title="International Cyanide Management Code">
       International Cyanide Management Code
      </a>
      , a program of best practice for the chemical’s use at gold mining operations, was developed under UNEP’s
      <a href="/wiki/Aegis" title="Aegis">
       aegis
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Environment_Programme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      UNEP headquarters were established in Nairobi, Kenya, in the late 1970s with a staff of 300, 100 of them professionals in a variety of fields and with a five‐year fund of more than $100 million. At the time, $40 million were pledged by the United States and the rest by 50 other nations.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Executive_Director">
       Executive Director
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Environment_Programme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Executive Director">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      UNEP's current Executive Director
      <a href="/wiki/Erik_Solheim" title="Erik Solheim">
       Erik Solheim
      </a>
      succeeded the previous director
      <a href="/wiki/Achim_Steiner" title="Achim Steiner">
       Achim Steiner
      </a>
      in 2016.
     </p>
     <p>
      The position was held for 17 years (1975–1992) by Dr.
      <a href="/wiki/Mostafa_Kamal_Tolba" title="Mostafa Kamal Tolba">
       Mostafa Kamal Tolba
      </a>
      , who was instrumental in bringing environmental considerations to the forefront of global thinking and action. Under his leadership, UNEP's most widely acclaimed success—the historic 1987 agreement to protect the ozone layer—the
      <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Protocol" title="Montreal Protocol">
       Montreal Protocol
      </a>
      was negotiated.
     </p>
     <p>
      During December 1972, the UN General Assembly unanimously elected
      <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Strong" title="Maurice Strong">
       Maurice Strong
      </a>
      to head UNEP. Also Secretary General of both the 1972 United Nations Conference on the Human Environment, which launched the world environment movement, and the 1992 Earth Summit, Strong has played a critical role is globalizing the environmental movement.
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">
       Structure
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Environment_Programme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Structure">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      UNEP's structure includes seven substantive Divisions:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Early Warning and Assessment (DEWA)
      </li>
      <li>
       Environmental Policy Implementation (DEPI)
      </li>
      <li>
       Technology, Industry and Economics (DTIE)
      </li>
      <li>
       Regional Cooperation (DRC)
      </li>
      <li>
       Environmental Law and Conventions (DELC)
      </li>
      <li>
       Communications and Public Information (DCPI)
      </li>
      <li>
       Global Environment Facility Coordination (DGEF)
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">
       Activities
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Environment_Programme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Activities">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      UNEP's main activities are related to:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">
        climate change
       </a>
       ;
       <ul>
        <li>
         including the
         <a href="/wiki/Territorial_Approach_to_Climate_Change" title="Territorial Approach to Climate Change">
          Territorial Approach to Climate Change
         </a>
         (TACC);
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
       disasters and conflicts;
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem_management" title="Ecosystem management">
        ecosystem management
       </a>
       ;
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Environmental_governance" title="Environmental governance">
        environmental governance
       </a>
       ;
      </li>
      <li>
       environment under review;
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Harmful_substance&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Harmful substance (page does not exist)">

       ; and
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Resource_efficiency" title="Resource efficiency">
        resource efficiency

      </li>
     </ul>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_achievements">
       Notable achievements
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Environment_Programme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Notable achievements">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      UNEP has registered several successes, such as the 1987
      <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Protocol" title="Montreal Protocol">
       Montreal Protocol
      </a>
      for limiting emissions of gases blamed for thinning the planet's protective ozone layer, or the 2012
      <a href="/wiki/Minamata_Convention_on_Mercury" title="Minamata Convention on Mercury">
       Minamata Convention
      </a>
      , a treaty to limit toxic mercury.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      UNEP has sponsored the development of solar loan programs, with attractive return rates, to buffer the initial deployment costs and entice consumers to consider and purchase solar PV systems. The most famous example is the
      <a href="/wiki/Indian_Solar_Loan_Programme" title="Indian Solar Loan Programme">
       solar loan program
      </a>
      sponsored by UNEP helped 100,000 people finance solar power systems in
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_power_in_India" title="Solar power in India">
       India
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Success in India's solar program has led to similar projects in other parts of the developing world like
      <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">
       Tunisia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">
       Morocco
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">
       Indonesia
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
       Mexico
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      UNEP sponsors the Marshlands project in the
      <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">
       Middle East
      </a>
      . In 2001, UNEP alerted the international community to the destruction of the Marshlands when it released satellite images showing that 90 percent of the Marshlands had already been lost. The UNEP "support for Environmental Management of the Iraqi Marshland" commenced in August 2004, in order to manage the Marshland area in an environmentally sound manner.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In order to ensure full participation of global communities, UNEP works in an inclusive fashion that brings on board different societal cohorts. UNEP has a programme for young people known as Tunza. Within this program are other projects like the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AEO_for_Youth" title="AEO for Youth">
       AEO for Youth
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       Glaciers shrinking
      </b>
     </p>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Further information:
      <a href="/wiki/Retreat_of_glaciers_since_1850" title="Retreat of glaciers since 1850">
       Retreat of glaciers since 1850
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Glacier" title="Glacier">
       Glaciers
      </a>
      are shrinking at record rates and many could disappear within decades, the U.N. Environment Programme said on March 16, 2008. The scientists measuring the health of almost 30 glaciers around the world found that ice loss reached record levels in 2006. On average, the glaciers shrank by 4.9 feet in 2006, the most recent year for which data are available. The most severe loss was recorded at
      <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">
       Norway
      </a>
      's Breidalblikkbrea glacier, which shrank 10.2 feet in 2006. Glaciers lost an average of about a foot of ice a year between 1980 and 1999. But since the turn of the millennium the average loss has increased to about 20 inches.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       Electric vehicles
      </b>
     </p>
     <p>
      At the fifth Magdeburg Environmental Forum held from 3–4 July 2008, in Magdeburg, Germany, UNEP and car manufacturer
      <a href="/wiki/Daimler_AG" title="Daimler AG">
       Daimler
      </a>
      called for the establishment of infrastructure for
      <a href="/wiki/Electric_vehicle" title="Electric vehicle">
       electric vehicles
      </a>
      . At this international conference, 250 high-ranking representatives from ce, politics and non-government organizations discussed solutions for future road transportation under the motto of "Sustainable Mobility–
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference_2009" title="United Nations Climate Change Conference 2009">
       the Post-2012 CO2 Agenda
      </a>
      ".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reports">
       Reports
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Environment_Programme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Reports">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      UNEP publishes many reports, atlases and newsletters. For instance, the fifth Global Environment Outlook (GEO-5) assessment is a comprehensive report on environment, development and human well-being, providing analysis and information for policy makers and the concerned public. One of many points in the GEO-5 warns that we are living far beyond our means. It notes that the human population is now so large that the amount of resources needed to sustain it exceeds what is available.
     </p>
     <p>
      In June 2010, a report from UNEP declared that a global shift towards a
      <a href="/wiki/Veganism" title="Veganism">
       vegan diet
      </a>
      was needed to save the world from hunger, fuel shortages and climate change.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reform">
       Reform
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Environment_Programme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reform">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main articles:
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=International_Sustainable_Energy_Agency&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="International Sustainable Energy Agency (page does not exist)">
       International Sustainable Energy Agency
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNEO" title="UNEO">
       UNEO
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IRENA" title="IRENA">
       IRENA
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Following the publication of
      <a href="/wiki/IPCC_Fourth_Assessment_Report" title="IPCC Fourth Assessment Report">
       Fourth Assessment Report
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_Panel_on_Climate_Change" title="Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change">
       Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change
      </a>
      (IPCC) in February 2007, a "Paris Call for Action" read out by French President
      <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Chirac" title="Jacques Chirac">
       Jacques Chirac
      </a>
      and supported by 46 countries, called for the United Nations Environment Programme to be replaced by a new and more powerful "United Nations Environment Organization (UNEO)", also called Global Environment Organisation now supported by French President
      <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Sarkozy" title="Nicolas Sarkozy">
       Nicolas Sarkozy
      </a>
      and German Chancellor
      <a href="/wiki/Angela_Merkel" title="Angela Merkel">
       Angela Merkel
      </a>
      , to be modelled on the
      <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">
       World Health Organization
      </a>
      . The 46 countries included the
      <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">
       European Union
      </a>
      nations, but notably did not include the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">
       Saudi Arabia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">
       Russia
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">
       China
      </a>
      , the top four emitters of greenhouse gases.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In December 2012, following the Rio+20 Summit, a decision by the General Assembly of the United Nations to '
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.unep.org/NEWSCENTRE/default.aspx?DocumentId=2700&amp;ArticleId=9363" rel="nofollow">
       strengthen and upgrade
      </a>
      ' the UN Environment Programme (UNEP) and establish universal membership of its governing body was confirmed.
     </p>
